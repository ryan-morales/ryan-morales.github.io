Who I Am and What I Do

I am currently an engineer-in-training with nearly 3 years of extensive engineering consulting experience working on the process piping design, implementation, and operation of combustion-based reactor systems for treating contaminated soils. I am a highly adaptable problem solver and I specialize in multiphysics simulations, heat transfer, and energy optimization.
My career goal is to contribute my technical skillset to problems within the fields of sustainability and climate change mitigation/adaptation. This is driven by a deep-seated interest in finding innovative and practical solutions to enhancing the efficiencies of the systems we rely on – getting more from less.
Outside of work I enjoy a wide-range of hobbies from hiking and portaging to building computers and generally tinkering. In the near future, I'm excited to plan trips to places I haven't yet ventured to in Canada, where I was proudly born and raised.
